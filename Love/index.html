Love

“How do I love thee? Let me count the ways.”

I once read somewhere that the oldest poems dwelled on merely three topics—food, religion, and romantic love. For I think, to be a poet is really to be a lover. In this section, each poem addresses a different type of love: Eros, Storge, Philia, and even, Agape. Not by intent but by luck, my selection spans the entire spectrum of what love encompasses.
